# Pre-Seed Funding Checklist

## Before You Start Fundraising

### 1. Product & Market

- [ ] Have a working MVP or prototype
- [ ] Have at least 10-20 early users or customers
- [ ] Can demonstrate product-market fit signals (retention, engagement, or revenue)
- [ ] Understand your target market deeply
- [ ] Have a clear value proposition

### 2. Team

- [ ] Have a co-founder (or strong justification for going solo)
- [ ] Team has relevant domain expertise
- [ ] Can demonstrate ability to execute
- [ ] Have clear equity split documented

### 3. Financials

- [ ] Know your burn rate
- [ ] Have a 12-18 month runway plan
- [ ] Understand unit economics (if applicable)
- [ ] Have basic financial projections
- [ ] Know how much you're raising and why

### 4. Legal & Corporate

- [ ] Company is incorporated (Delaware C-Corp recommended)
- [ ] Cap table is clean and documented
- [ ] IP is assigned to the company
- [ ] Have basic legal documents (founders' agreement, etc.)
- [ ] Understand SAFE vs. priced round trade-offs

### 5. Pitch Materials

- [ ] Have a compelling pitch deck (10-15 slides)
- [ ] Can articulate the problem clearly
- [ ] Can explain your solution
- [ ] Have a clear go-to-market strategy
- [ ] Know your competition and differentiation
- [ ] Have a vision for the future

### 6. Network & Introductions

- [ ] Have warm introductions to investors
- [ ] Have a list of target investors (angels, pre-seed funds)
- [ ] Have advisors or mentors who can make introductions
- [ ] Are active in relevant startup communities

### 7. Traction Metrics

- [ ] Can show growth (users, revenue, or engagement)
- [ ] Have customer testimonials or case studies
- [ ] Can demonstrate momentum
- [ ] Have a clear path to next milestone

### 8. Mindset

- [ ] Understand that fundraising is hard and takes time
- [ ] Are prepared for rejection
- [ ] Have conviction in your idea
- [ ] Are ready to commit full-time

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Raising too early**: Don't raise before you have something to show
2. **Raising too much**: Pre-seed is typically $250K-$1M
3. **Wrong investors**: Target investors who invest at your stage
4. **Unrealistic valuation**: Be reasonable about your valuation
5. **No traction**: Investors want to see progress, not just ideas

## Timeline

- **Preparation**: 2-4 weeks
- **Outreach**: 2-4 weeks
- **Meetings**: 4-8 weeks
- **Due diligence & closing**: 2-4 weeks
- **Total**: 3-4 months typically

## Key Documents Needed

1. Pitch deck
2. Executive summary (1-2 pages)
3. Financial model
4. Cap table
5. Term sheet (when you get an offer)
